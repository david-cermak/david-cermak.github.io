⚠️Clickbait: My PQC HOT take

Here's an interesting and beginner-friendly systematization of PQC adoption:
👉 https://arxiv.org/html/2606.04669v1
This paper makes an important point: PQC adoption is not just about replacing RSA/ECC with lattice-based schemes.
Instead, it argues that successful migration spans three dimensions: *H*umans; *O*rganisations; *T*echnology (HOT)

I particularly liked the clear taxonomy, and the focus on education, tooling, governance, and migration challenges.
Most discussions focus on algorithms and performance. This paper asks whether organisations, processes, and *developers are actually ready *for the transition.

*But*🤔, I'm not entirely convinced...
NIST only finalized the first 3 standards in Aug 2024, FIPS 206 is still in draft, and many deployments remain experimental. 
Is the focus on tooling, libraries, interoperability, and implementation experience the right priority today? Wouldn't the organisational frameworks naturally follow once the tech part stabilizes?

🗨️ WYT? Do we really need to invest (equally) in governance, organisational change, and developer education today, or should we first let the technology stabilize?
