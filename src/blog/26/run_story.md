⚠️Personal story: Weekend running report

After ~100 tech posts, I wanted to share something personal... no architecture lessons, no leadership stories.

This weekend I took part in "Jesenicka-60": 57 km race across *Jeseníky* (mountains in CZ). After 1year+ without serious running (thanks to some/minor injuries: my Achilles' heel = my Achilles' heel), I just wanted to get back to one of my favorite events. No big ambitions, just wanted to finish.

Results:
🏃 57 km, 6:58:33 total time, 2120m elevation
📈 Average pace: 7:20 min/km; 14th place out of 106 participants

Stories:
* Survived😄, including my achille's tendon!
* 7:20 min/km pace doesn't sound much like running... but with ~2km climb I'll take it. Bonus: finished under 7 hours.
* At around 20 km, my hydration bag got a leak... only noticed because my backpack suddenly felt really wet. Solution: drink as much as possible, dump the rest, and switch to a classic bottle at the next refreshment station.
* My running poles bent during the race. Not a big issue, they just refused to fold and had to stay in my hands.

👏 Big thanks to the organizers! Great course, perfect weather, and nice conditions.
And most importantly: I'm *back in the game. *Looking forward to 2027.
